The relationship operating system

The private memory and instinct of a great connector, in your pocket. We win the in-person, calendar-packed operator with frictionless real-world capture and a briefing the moment before the handshake.

The loop we own — design every surface to spin it

No incumbent owns this end-to-end. Cards win the exchange but forget the person; CRMs remember but stumble in-person; note-takers capture calls but bolt CRM onto a meeting tool.

What it is

A mobile-first relationship operating system for high-stakes operators — people whose network is high-value and whose memory of it is failing. Recall is the hero, not storage.

Who it's for

Product targets: C-suite relationship-builders, front-line AEs, and founders/investors.

Launch graph: founders + investors + execs who meet each other — dense, recurring peer scenes where reciprocity is native. Reps arrive later via expansion + CRM sync once the graph has density.

The three hero moments — design these first

  1. 1Voice-to-card. Speak a 10-second note; on-device AI returns a structured person record. This is the entire pitch.
  2. 2Just-in-time briefing. "Brief me on Marcus before my 3pm" — history, open threads, talking points, shared connections. Hands-free via Siri. The moment a C-level feels superhuman.
  3. 3Reciprocal share-back. A no-app web card the recipient values immediately, then an invite to claim & control their profile. The conversion mechanic.

Core flows to design (v1 surface)

Onboarding · <90s, one magic moment Capture · voice + tap/NameDrop/NFC/QR/Wallet Person card & relationship timeline Briefing surface + Siri / App Intents Follow-up drafting & proactive nudges Recipient web card + profile-claim Scene / event seeding mode

Design principles

  • Recall is the hero. Lead with the briefing, not the database.
  • Near-zero-friction capture. If the 10-second habit has any friction, nothing else matters.
  • Visible privacy. Make the on-device / cloud line legible. Consent is a first-class surface with granular toggles and an honest "stayed on this device" indicator — a nervous C-level must see the privacy.
  • Premium, exec-grade restraint. The share-back must feel like a gift from a high-status person, never referral spam. Reputation protection is the growth strategy.
Sequencing · loop-first

Build the viral loop early — with one hard rule

Single-player capture + recall stays a MUST. It's the fuel the loop spins on: people share and capture only because remembering gives them value with zero other users. No substrate, no loop.

Seed one dense, recurring scene (the seed-stage SF founder/investor circuit), not one-off events. The recipient is the hard side with no inherent pain — give them standalone value (a free, always-current identity card + their own relationship brain). Reciprocity is the bonus, not the pitch.

North star

Time-to-second-capture / K-factor — not DAU.

Open risk — falsify before fully engineering virality

The riskiest assumption: that a converted recipient becomes an active sender at a compounding rate. The "someone keeps notes on me" framing can also read as creepy to the very privacy-anxious execs we court.

Falsify cheaply first: saturate one scene, hand-seed 10–30 superconnectors, fake claim/enrichment/briefing by hand, instrument the full funnel, and set the second-capture threshold before running it. If a dense, high-reciprocity scene can't clear the bar, switch to Plan B — single-player + B2B land-and-expand. The loop is a bet on velocity, not on the company's existence.

Technology spine

On-device by default via Apple's Foundation Models (~3B LLM) for extraction, summarization & structured output; on-device speech for transcription; App Intents to ride the conversational-Siri wave. Hybrid cloud — explicit per-action consent — only for web enrichment and heavy lifting.

Monetization

Freemium for acquisition. Revenue engine: exec-led B2B land-and-expand — the C-level adopts, the org buys. Distinctive layer: one-tap thoughtful gestures with margin on the gesture. Avoid aggregate data products — they undercut the trust positioning.
